Feature Guide

Everything this app can do

Budget Tracker

Complete app overview
How data is organised

All data is stored month-by-month in your browser's local storage — nothing is sent to any server. Each month is independent. Use the arrow buttons in the header to move between months, or type directly in the month field.

Global data (net worth assets, subscriptions, debt accounts, savings goals) persists across all months since these represent ongoing positions rather than monthly snapshots.

Backup & restore

Use Backup JSON from the menu to download a full snapshot of all your data. Use Restore JSON to reload it. Do this before clearing data or switching devices.

Export CSV outputs the current month's income, bills, and expenses as a spreadsheet. Import CSV lets you bulk-load that data back in.

Copying between months

At the start of a new month, use Copy month data from the menu. A picker lets you choose any previously saved month as the source. Income, bills, and categories copy across; individual expense entries do not, so each month starts clean.

Undo & clearing data

Most destructive actions (deleting entries, removing income sources, clearing bills) can be undone with the ↩ Undo toast that appears at the bottom-left. It lasts a few seconds before expiring.

Clear all data in the menu permanently erases every month. This cannot be undone — backup first.

Refresh

The Refresh button on the Overview tab recalculates all figures across every section. Useful if numbers ever appear stale after switching months.

Dark mode

Toggle dark mode from the menu. Your preference is saved and persists across sessions.

The floating pill in the bottom-right always shows your current net balance (income minus bills minus reserve-adjusted expenses). It turns green when positive and red when negative.

Overview

Monthly snapshot & income management
KPI cards

The five summary cards show Total Income, Bills, Expenses, Net Remaining, and Net Worth at a glance. Net Remaining is income minus bills minus the reserve-adjusted expense figure.

Income sources

Add named income sources (salary, freelance, rental income, etc.) with their monthly amounts. The bar chart scales each source relative to the largest.

To edit an amount, click directly on the number next to a source — it becomes an input field. Press Enter or click away to save. The ✕ button removes the source entirely.

Expense breakdown

A read-only breakdown of every bill category and expense category combined, sorted by spend. Useful for spotting which areas consume the most budget.

Month-over-month chart

Scrolling down reveals a bar chart comparing income, bills, expenses, and net across all saved months. Hover over bars to see exact values.

Customize dashboard

The ⊞ Customize Dashboard button lets you show or hide individual KPI cards to reduce clutter if some sections aren't relevant to you.

Net Remaining shows a hint line when a reserve is set on the Expenses page — e.g. "$500 reserved · $320 unspent" — so you always know how much of the earmark is still available.

Bills

Fixed monthly obligations
Adding & editing bills

Each bill has a payee, category, actual amount, budget amount, and payment method (Visa, Check, Draft, Other). The pencil icon on each row opens the edit modal.

The Diff column shows budget minus actual — green when under budget, red when over.

Marking bills paid

Toggle the checkbox on any row to mark it paid. Paid rows dim out. The summary bar at the top tracks how many are paid, how many remain, and the total still owed.

Copy last month's bills at the start of each month using the header menu. Only the structure copies — paid status resets so you start fresh.

Expenses

Variable day-to-day spending
Categories & cards

Expenses are organised into categories, each shown as a card. Add a category with + Add Category. Each card shows the category name, a budget cap, total spent, a progress bar, and the list of individual entries below.

If a card has more than 4 entries the list scrolls. A faint hint at the top of the list tells you how many entries there are.

Adding entries

Type a description and amount at the bottom of any card and press Enter or + to add it. After adding, the cursor returns to the description field so you can keep entering items quickly.

As you type a description, previously used descriptions for that category appear as suggestions — click or use arrow keys to select one.

Net Reserve

The Net Reserve field at the top of the page lets you earmark a fixed amount for expenses. This full amount is always deducted from Net Remaining on the Overview, regardless of how much you've actually spent — so you can see your true discretionary balance at any time.

The Expenses KPI on the Overview still shows your actual spend. The reserve only affects the Net Remaining calculation.

Editing & deleting entries

The pencil icon on each entry opens an edit modal where you can change the description or amount. The ✕ button deletes the entry — this is undoable via the toast.

The ＋ floating button (bottom-right) opens a Quick Add modal — a fast way to log an expense without scrolling to the right category card.

Annual Expenses

Irregular & yearly costs
What belongs here

Track expenses that don't fit neatly into a monthly budget — car registration, insurance premiums, holiday spending, home repairs. Enter a prior year amount and this year's amount to track year-over-year change.

Year-over-year diff

The Diff column shows this year minus last year. Green means you spent less; red means more. The totals row at the bottom summarises all three columns.

Savings

Goal-based savings tracking
Creating savings buckets

Each savings card represents a separate pot — emergency fund, vacation, down payment, etc. Set a target amount and the progress bar fills as you add to the balance. The percentage complete is shown alongside the current balance.

Adding & withdrawing

Use the + Add input on each card to deposit an amount. Use the − Withdraw input to record a withdrawal. Both are logged and the balance updates immediately.

Savings balances persist across months as part of global data, so your running total is always up to date regardless of which month you're viewing.

Debt

Payoff tracking & strategy simulator
Tracking debt accounts

Add each debt (credit card, loan, mortgage) with its current balance, interest rate, minimum payment, and type. The card shows balance, interest, and a progress bar from original balance to zero.

Payoff simulator

Below the debt cards, the Payoff Simulator calculates how long it will take to eliminate all debts. Enter an extra monthly payment to see how additional money accelerates payoff.

Switch between Avalanche (highest interest first — least total interest) and Snowball (lowest balance first — fastest psychological wins) strategies.

Goals

Financial milestones & tracking
Setting goals

Goals are named milestones with a type (savings, debt payoff, income, spending reduction), a target amount, a current amount, and an optional target date. The progress bar turns green when on track, amber when behind.

Updating progress

Use the update input on each goal card to log new progress. The app calculates how much you need per month to hit your target date, and whether your current pace will get you there in time.

Net Worth

Assets, liabilities & trend over time
Assets & liabilities

Add assets (bank accounts, investments, property, vehicles) and liabilities (mortgages, loans, credit card balances) with their current values. Net worth is the difference. Update values any time — the summary bar at the top always shows total assets, total liabilities, and net.

Net worth trend

Each time you update your net worth, a snapshot is saved. The chart below the inputs shows how your net worth has changed over time, with bars coloured by whether the value increased or decreased from the previous snapshot.

Net worth is also shown as a KPI card on the Overview page, so you always have it in context alongside your monthly figures.

What If

Scenario modelling sandbox
How it works

Create hypothetical scenarios without touching your real data. Each scenario lets you adjust income, add or remove expenses, and change bill amounts. The app recalculates your net position for that scenario in real time.

Use cases

Model a salary increase, a new loan payment, moving to a cheaper apartment, or dropping a subscription. Compare the resulting net figures side by side to make more informed decisions.

Retirement

Long-term projection calculator
Inputs

Enter your current age, planned retirement age, life expectancy, current savings, monthly contribution, and expected annual return. The calculator projects both the accumulation phase (saving) and the drawdown phase (spending in retirement).

Reading the output

The accumulation table shows projected balance year by year until retirement. The drawdown table then shows annual withdrawals at your specified monthly spend until funds are exhausted or you reach your life expectancy. A summary shows whether your savings will last.

All projections are estimates based on constant rates. Adjust the return rate and monthly spend to explore best- and worst-case scenarios.

Subscriptions

Recurring service management
Tracking subscriptions

Add subscriptions with name, provider, cost, billing period, renewal date, category (Streaming, Insurance, Membership, Service), and a personal value assessment (Essential, Worth It, Marginal, Consider Cancelling).

Cards are grouped by type and show total monthly cost per group. The header shows overall monthly and annual totals.

Import from bills

When adding a new subscription, the Import from existing bill dropdown lets you pre-fill the form from a bill already entered this month. The app infers the subscription type from keywords in the payee name.

Use the value assessment field honestly — subscriptions marked "Consider Cancelling" are visually flagged so they stand out during your monthly review.
